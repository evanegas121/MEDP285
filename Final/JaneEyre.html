Jane Eyre by Charlotte Brontë


Orphaned as a child, Jane has felt an outcast her whole young life. Her courage is tested once again when she arrives at Thornfield Hall, where she has been hired by the brooding, proud Edward Rochester to care for his ward Adèle. Jane finds herself drawn to his troubled yet kind spirit. She falls in love. Hard.

But there is a terrifying secret inside the gloomy, forbidding Thornfield Hall. Is Rochester hiding from Jane? Will Jane be left heartbroken and exiled once again?





Review :

I'm definitely a lot biased since this book holds a lot of high school nostalgia for me. This is one of those classics that really make me want to make tea and pretend I'm some governess in the Victorian era. I think Jane can resonate with a lot of readers during her childhood and questioning those in power. Her childhood at the boarding school was quite heartwrenching.I loved all(most) of the characters though I wished Adèle had more apearance time. I really like the relationship between Mr.Rochester and Jane (looking back though Mr.Rochestor has a lot of red flags).
